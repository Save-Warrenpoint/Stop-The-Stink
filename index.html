STOP THE STINK

Get RE-GEN OUT of Warrenpoint

Our Community is Under Siege

For too long, Re-Gen has used Warrenpoint Port to store massive quantities of waste. This isn't just a nuisance—it is a health and environmental crisis that has been documented for years.

  • Non-Compliance: Reports have highlighted "non-compliant" waste operations, including compromised bales and waste stored outside licensed areas.
  • Economic Impact: The stench has disrupted local festivals, harmed tourism, and affected local businesses.
  • Health & Wellbeing: Residents are forced to live with "nauseating" smells and infestations of flies, unable to open windows.

The Latest: Authority Demands Change

Recent reports have confirmed the severity of the situation. We demand that the authorities listen to the following facts:

  • Storage Ban Required: The Harbour Authority has called for an end to the storage of Refuse Derived Fuel (RDF) on the ground, insisting it be moved directly onto ships.
  • Unresolved Issues: Despite claims that RDF has been removed, the smell persists. Residents and independent footage suggest that odorous waste remains a major issue.
  • Lack of Oversight: The NIEA and DAERA have been accused of failing to enforce license conditions, with some reports showing the annual waste allowance was quadrupled despite ongoing complaints.

"We will not consider re-engagement until there is a complete and permanent end to all RDF activities at Warrenpoint Port."
